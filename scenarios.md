# Scenario Planning

## Best Case (2–6 weeks disruption)
- Short-term shortages (fuel, imported goods)
- Panic buying cycles
- Supply stabilizes gradually

### Recommendation
- Minimum: 2–4 weeks of food
- ~75,000–150,000 kcal stored

---

## Medium Scenario (2–6 months disruption)
- Persistent shipping bottlenecks
- Reduced supermarket stock variety
- Rising food prices
- Fuel rationing likely

### Recommendation
- 3–6 months buffer
- ~450,000–900,000 kcal stored

---

## Worst Case (6–18+ months, LOW PROBABILITY)
- Long-term geopolitical escalation
- Severe import reduction
- Rationing systems introduced
- Reliance on domestic production

### Recommendation
- 6–12+ months buffer
- Focus on:
  - Staples (rice, oats, flour)
  - Preservation skills
  - Community resilience

---

## Key Insight
Australia produces food domestically — **calorie collapse unlikely**, but:
- Distribution + fuel = bottleneck
- Variety and convenience foods = disappear first
